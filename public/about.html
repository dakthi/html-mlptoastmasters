About MLP London Bridge Speakers

Building confident speakers and leaders in the heart of London since 2005

Our Story

MLP London Bridge Speakers is a vibrant Toastmasters club serving the London Bridge area. We're part of District 91, Area L23, and have been helping members overcome their fear of public speaking and develop essential leadership skills in a supportive, friendly environment.

We're part of Toastmasters International, a global organization with over 16,200 clubs worldwide. Our club meets at St Christopher's Inn, 121 Borough High Street, London SE1 1NP on the 1st, 3rd, and 5th Tuesday of each month at 6:30 PM.

In September 2025, we celebrated a special milestone anniversary and recently secured permanent use of the private room at St Christopher's Inn. Our members actively participate in contests, with recent winners progressing to Area level competitions in both Humorous Speech and Table Topics categories.

What We Offer

Our club offers a structured educational program with 6 different Pathways to choose from, each designed to develop specific communication and leadership skills.

From your Ice Breaker speech to advanced projects, you'll receive constructive feedback and support from experienced members at every step of your journey.

Our Community

Our members come from diverse backgrounds and industries, creating a unique networking opportunity while you develop your skills. We actively welcome new members and guests at every meeting.

Whether you're a complete beginner or an experienced speaker, you'll find a welcoming community ready to support your growth. Recent new members have praised the supportive atmosphere and the structured approach to learning.

Why Join Us?

In today's world, communication is everything. Whether you're looking to advance your career, become a more confident presenter, or simply overcome your fear of public speaking, Toastmasters provides the tools and support you need.

Our members have gone on to win speech contests, lead teams, start businesses, and achieve personal goals they never thought possible. The skills you learn at Toastmasters extend far beyond public speaking - they're life skills that benefit every aspect of your personal and professional life.

Real member stories

Hear from our members about their journeys (from our November 18, 2025 meeting)

Nick - MLP Member, 10 Years

"When I started Toastmasters 10 years ago I was socially introverted. I couldn't even look people in the eye. I shook and sweated when I talked to more than two people."

"I remember I did a table topic and I was on stage for 10 seconds just shaking and mumbling and then everyone clapped for me. I realised that was Toastmasters - encouragement. My confidence has continued to grow year on year and I have now delivered over a hundred speeches and taken up many leadership roles in my club and above."

"Whatever you do, even if you just go on stage for only 10 seconds as a guest and mumble, it's a start and it's a good accomplishment."

Emily - Nearly 10 years

"I went to a face-to-face meeting nearly ten years ago and everyone's clapping as they go on stage, and I was just shaking my head, like 'what is this?' But then you get used to that part, or you embrace that part, and realise that it is a nice thing to do, it's encouraging to everyone."

"Everyone genuinely is welcome, there's no cliques. The fact that we have guests and new people every meeting allows for that, so it becomes its own little community."

Matt - Experienced member

"I remember when I first went along to Toastmasters, it was a couple of times where I really had to force myself to actually go into the meeting. I think everyone has that real big hurdle to actually overcome and get started with it."

"The thing that really surprised me was having that ability to be able to find things to say, which was something I used to really struggle with. It does have a really big impact on how you come across at work and how good you are at communicating."

What you can do as guests vs members

Compare the benefits and opportunities available to guests and members

As a Guest

What you can do:

  • ✓ Attend meetings and observe prepared speeches
  • ✓ Participate in Table Topics (impromptu speaking)
  • ✓ Experience the meeting structure and atmosphere
  • ✓ Meet members and ask questions
  • ✓ Visit up to 3 times before deciding to join
  • ✓ Network with professionals from diverse backgrounds

Cost:

Free - no cost or obligation to attend as a guest

As a Member

Everything guests can do, plus:

  • ✓ Deliver prepared speeches and receive evaluations
  • ✓ Access to all 6 Pathways learning programmes
  • ✓ Assigned mentor to guide your progress
  • ✓ Participate in speech contests and competitions
  • ✓ Take on leadership roles in the club
  • ✓ Access to Toastmasters International resources
  • ✓ Earn awards and recognition for achievements
  • ✓ Vote in club elections and decisions

Cost:

Membership dues apply (covers Toastmasters International fees, educational materials, and club expenses)
