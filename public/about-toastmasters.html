Understanding Toastmasters

Learn the difference between Toastmasters International and individual clubs like ours

Many people confuse Toastmasters International (the global organization) with individual Toastmasters clubs (local member-run groups). Understanding this distinction helps clarify what we offer and how we operate.

Toastmasters International

toastmasters.org →

What It Is

A global non-profit educational organization founded in 1924

What They Do

  • Develops educational programs and materials
  • Provides the Pathways learning experience
  • Maintains membership standards and guidelines
  • Organizes district and international conferences
  • Supports 16,000+ clubs in 143 countries

Their Role

Headquarters and central organization that provides resources, curriculum, and support to all member clubs worldwide

MLP London Bridge Speakers

mlptoastmasters.org →

What We Are

A local, member-run club (Club #00760422) affiliated with Toastmasters International

What We Do

  • Hold regular meetings (Tuesdays 6:30pm)
  • Deliver speeches and receive feedback
  • Practice leadership through club roles
  • Create a supportive learning environment
  • Use Toastmasters International materials

Our Role

A community of individuals meeting locally to improve communication and leadership skills using the Toastmasters program

Key Differences

Scope

International: Global organization with 345,000+ members

Our Club: Local group of 20-30 members in London Bridge

Purpose

International: Develops programs, materials, and standards

Our Club: Delivers meetings and learning experiences

Website

International: toastmasters.org (official HQ)

Our Club: mlptoastmasters.org (our local club)

How They Work Together

1

Membership

When you join MLP London Bridge Speakers, you also become a member of Toastmasters International. Your dues are split between our club and the international organization.

2

Educational Materials

We use the Pathways learning experience and other resources developed by Toastmasters International to structure our meetings and educational programs.

3

Standards & Support

Toastmasters International provides guidelines, training, and support to ensure all clubs maintain quality standards and deliver consistent value to members.

4

Competitions & Events

Our club participates in speech contests organized at district and international levels, connecting with the broader Toastmasters community.

What This Means for You

When you visit mlptoastmasters.org (this site), you're learning about our specific club in London Bridge. When you visit toastmasters.org, you'll find information about the global organization, how to find clubs worldwide, and resources for all members.
