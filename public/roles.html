Club Roles & Responsibilities

Learn about the leadership opportunities and meeting roles that make our club successful

Every role in Toastmasters provides valuable leadership experience and skill development. Whether serving on the executive committee or taking a meeting role, you'll build confidence, practice new skills, and contribute to the success of our club.

For official role guides, visit Toastmasters.org Meeting Roles

Club Roles Overview

Executive Committee Meeting Roles Support Roles
President

Chief executive officer of the club, responsible for overall club success.

Time: 4-6 hours/week

Vice President Education (VPE)

Oversees all educational programs and member progress.

Time: 3-5 hours/week

Vice President Membership (VPM)

Responsible for attracting and retaining members.

Time: 3-4 hours/week

Vice President Public Relations (VPPR)

Manages the club's public image and promotes the club.

Time: 3-4 hours/week

Secretary

Maintains club records and manages correspondence.

Time: 2-3 hours/week

Treasurer

Manages club finances and collects dues.

Time: 2-3 hours/week

Sergeant at Arms (SAA)

Ensures the meeting space is properly set up and manages club resources.

Time: 2-3 hours/week
Toastmaster of the Day

Hosts and facilitates the club meeting.

Time: 2-3 hours preparation

General Evaluator

Oversees the evaluation portion of the meeting.

Time: 1-2 hours preparation

Speech Evaluator

Provides constructive feedback on prepared speeches.

Time: 30-60 min preparation

Table Topics Master

Conducts the impromptu speaking session.

Time: 30-60 min preparation

Timer

Tracks and signals time for all speakers.

Time: Minimal preparation

Grammarian

Tracks language use and introduces Word of the Day.

Time: 30-60 min preparation

Ah-Counter

Tracks verbal pauses and filler words.

Time: Minimal preparation
Mentor

Guides new members through their first months in Toastmasters.

Time: 1-2 hours/month per mentee

Webmaster

Maintains and updates the club website and online presence.

Time: 1-2 hours/week

Why Take on a Role?

Build Leadership Skills

Every role develops specific leadership competencies that transfer to your professional and personal life.

Practice in Safety

Make mistakes and learn in a supportive environment where everyone is focused on growth.

Connect with Members

Taking roles helps you build relationships and become an integral part of the club community.

Accelerate Your Progress

Many roles count toward Pathways leadership projects and help you complete levels faster.

Ready to Take on a Role?

Talk to our Vice President Education about volunteering for a role at your next meeting. Every journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step!
